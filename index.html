Background

Generative video tools are increasingly being applied in healthcare workflows, including:

  • Patient education
  • Workflow optimization
  • Professional communication

Challenge: Many healthcare professionals lack familiarity, confidence, and positive attitudes toward these tools, limiting their effectiveness.

Study Aim: To evaluate the impact of a targeted educational program on participants' knowledge, attitudes, and skills.

Summary of Work

📊 Study Design

Pre-test and post-test design was adopted to assess the effectiveness of the educational intervention.

🔍 Pre-Course Assessment

Participants were surveyed to evaluate their baseline:

  • Familiarity with the tools
  • Perceptions of their professional value
  • Confidence in practical application

🎓 Course Components

  • Structured instruction
  • Hands-on practice
  • Demonstrations of generative video tools in real-world scenarios

📈 Analysis

Post-course questionnaire analyzed changes in three domains:

Knowledge Attitudes Skills

Discussion and Conclusion

✅ Key Findings

This study demonstrates that structured educational interventions effectively enhance healthcare professionals' acceptance and ability to use generative video tools.

🎯 Implications

Improvements in knowledge, attitudes, and skills suggest that targeted training can help overcome barriers to technology adoption.

💡 Importance of Hands-on Learning

The findings highlight the importance of incorporating hands-on opportunities in professional development programs to bridge the gap between emerging technologies and practical applications.

🔮 Future Directions

Future studies should further explore the long-term impacts and scalability of such programs across diverse professional settings.

Take Home Message(s)

  • • Targeted training boosts healthcare professionals' readiness to adopt generative video tools.
  • • Hands-on learning bridges technology and practical application in healthcare.
